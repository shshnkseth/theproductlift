Rebuilding a Restaurant OS: POS and Customer App, V1 to V2

Summer needed a generational product upgrade across high-pressure POS workflows and customer-facing experiences. The core challenge was consistency at speed across multiple restaurant locations.

2021–2023 · Delivered by The Product Lift
Restaurant OS Design System Multi-Surface UX B2B Product V1 → V2

Client Summary

The product operated across 30+ restaurants with rising interface debt. Teams struggled with fragmented patterns and feature-level inconsistency, slowing delivery and training.

Project Summary

We audited V1, defined a shared design system, then rebuilt critical workflows for POS and customer surfaces in parallel. The system was designed for operational speed under real-world service constraints.

What We Delivered

  • Product audit and gap analysis across surfaces
  • Design token architecture and reusable components
  • Flow redesign for ordering and fulfillment tasks
  • Customer-facing app pattern unification
  • Developer documentation for production rollout

Outcome

V2 shipped with cleaner interactions and stronger consistency, reducing implementation friction and helping teams roll features faster across locations.
