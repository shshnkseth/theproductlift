Toast (now Summer): Redesigning Restaurant Experience Across POS and App

Toast (now rebranded as Summer) is a Hyderabad-based startup focused on simplifying restaurant management and elevating customer experience through technology. I joined as a freelance product designer to reimagine the full ecosystem, from the restaurant Point-of-Sale (POS) backend to the customer-facing mobile app.

Executive Snapshot

Toast needed a full-system redesign across operations and customer touchpoints, where speed and clarity during peak service mattered more than feature volume.

Business Context

30+ restaurants, fragmented workflows, and inconsistent service behavior across POS and customer app experiences.

Design Objective

Reduce operational friction for staff while making customer ordering simpler, faster, and confidence-driven.

Scope

POS UX, mobile app UX, design system foundations, flow strategy, and execution support through rollout.

Role and Timeline

Freelance Product Designer across two phases from Dec 2019 to Aug 2021.

Research to Problem Definition

Field observations showed that failure points happened under pressure, not in ideal demo flows. The highest-cost moments were in rush-hour order handling, kitchen sync, and billing corrections.

  • Shadow sessions: Real shift observations across ordering, serving, kitchen, and cash counter workflows.
  • Operational friction map: Identified repetitive actions and slow handoffs across staff roles.
  • Primary pain points: Excess taps, low status visibility, high training dependency, weak inventory feedback.
  • Design mandate: Build for peak-hour behavior first, then optimize edge polish.

System Redesign Highlights

Order Layer

Unified dine-in, takeaway, and delivery into one creation flow with real-time kitchen state sync.

Table Operations

Live table map with quick state transitions and role-aware actions for faster staff coordination.

Billing Flows

Split-bill, mixed-mode payments, and correction-safe edits to reduce end-of-meal delays.

Inventory Visibility

Recipe-linked stock logic and low-stock indicators surfaced inside ordering context, not buried in admin.

Product Principles That Drove Decisions

  • Speed first: Critical actions designed to complete in under 3 taps.
  • Cognitive simplicity: Language and controls optimized for fast shift onboarding.
  • Error tolerance: Reversible actions and status feedback in high-pressure moments.
  • Resilience: Offline-safe behavior with later sync to protect service continuity.

Customer App Improvements

The app strategy focused on removing wait-time friction while keeping ordering confidence high across dine-in, takeaway, and delivery behaviors.

  • Dine-in: QR-based table journey, collaborative ordering, and split billing without staff dependency.
  • Takeaway and delivery: Cleaner reorder loops, prep estimates, and dietary context where decisions happen.
  • Engagement layer: Chef stories and recipe narratives to improve non-transactional retention.

Impact Snapshot

-66% Onboarding time reduced from ~90 minutes to under 30 minutes for new staff.
-22% Table turnover time reduced through faster order and table-state operations.
10,000+ Monthly active users within 3 months of beta release across 30+ restaurants.

Operational and Product Gains

Key Takeaways

  • Hospitality UX wins when designed around shift pressure, not idealized user journeys.
  • Operational clarity for staff directly improves customer experience quality.
  • Small interaction decisions in billing and table handoff have outsized business impact.
  • Design systems become adoption tools when they map to role-specific reality.
